Frequently Asked Questions

Technical details and common questions about this PDF compressor.

How does Ghostscript WASM compression work in a browser?
Ghostscript — a C PDF processing engine that has existed since the 1980s — has been compiled to WebAssembly using Emscripten. The full real Ghostscript binary runs inside your browser tab. Your PDF is written to an in-memory virtual filesystem, Ghostscript processes it using its pdfwrite device, and the output is read back and downloaded to your device. No server is involved at any step.
Why is Ghostscript better than canvas-based compression?
Canvas-based compressors render every page to a bitmap and reassemble them into a PDF. This converts all text and vector graphics into blurry pixels — the result is not selectable, not searchable, and looks poor when zoomed. Ghostscript works at the stream level: it recompresses only embedded raster images, while keeping all text, fonts, and vector content as proper PDF objects.
Are my files uploaded to a server?
No. The Ghostscript WASM binary (~16 MB) is loaded from a CDN once and runs entirely inside your browser. Your PDF file itself never leaves your device — it is read into browser memory, processed locally by the WASM engine, and the output is downloaded directly. No network request is made with your file content.
Why does the first compression take longer?
The Ghostscript WebAssembly binary is approximately 16 MB. Your browser downloads and compiles it the first time. Subsequent uses in the same session reuse the loaded engine instantly. Your browser also caches the WASM file, so future visits are much faster.
Will compressed PDFs have a watermark?
No. Ghostscript does not add watermarks or branding. The library is open source (AGPL-3.0) and this tool is free — there is no reason to watermark your output. You receive a completely clean compressed PDF.
Which preset should I use?
For email and general web sharing, use eBook — best balance of quality and compression. For maximum size reduction (strict upload limits), use Screen. For printing at home or the office, use Printer. For PDFs going to a commercial print house, use Prepress.
