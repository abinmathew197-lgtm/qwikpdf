When Should You Convert PDF to PNG?

PNG is the right choice whenever quality matters more than file size.

📄 Text-Heavy Documents

Reports, invoices, legal documents, and contracts — any PDF where crisp, readable text is the priority. PNG keeps every letter perfectly sharp; JPG can make text look slightly soft or blurry.

📊 Charts, Diagrams & Technical Drawings

Engineering drawings, flowcharts, data visualizations, and architecture diagrams need pixel-perfect lines and sharp edges. PNG's lossless compression ensures no detail is lost.

🖥️ Web & App Development

Use PNG when embedding PDF page images into websites or apps where transparency or pixel-perfect sharpness is required, such as product sheets, UI mockups, or interactive viewers.

🖼️ Image Editing & Processing

If you'll open the PNG in Photoshop, GIMP, or Figma for further editing, always start with PNG. Editing a JPG repeatedly causes accumulating quality loss — PNG avoids this entirely.

🖨️ High-Quality Printing

Convert to 300 DPI PNG for professional printing workflows. Lossless compression ensures no compression artifacts appear at large print sizes, unlike JPG which can show banding in flat areas.

📁 Archiving & Long-Term Storage

For archiving PDF pages as images, PNG is the preferred format. Since it's lossless, a PNG you create today will look identical 10 years from now — no progressive quality degradation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything about converting PDF files to PNG images online for free.

How do I convert a PDF to PNG online for free?
Upload your PDF, choose your DPI setting, then click Save as PNG on any individual page. You can also download all pages at once using the Download All as ZIP button. No account, software installation, or server upload required — everything runs in your browser.
Is PNG better than JPG for PDF conversion?
PNG is better for PDF pages that contain text, diagrams, charts, or line art, because it uses lossless compression — zero quality loss. JPG is better when file size is the priority and the page contains mostly photographic content. For most business documents, reports, and presentations, PNG will give you sharper, more professional results.
What DPI should I use when converting PDF to PNG?
For web and screen use, 96–150 DPI produces excellent results at reasonable file sizes. For print-quality output, use 200–300 DPI. 300 DPI is the professional printing standard — this is what you need for high-quality prints, large-format output, or archival storage.
Are my PDF files uploaded to a server?
No. All PDF to PNG conversion happens entirely in your browser using PDF.js — a Mozilla-developed open source PDF rendering library. Your files never leave your device. This makes it completely safe for confidential, legal, medical, and personal documents. No account, no tracking, no data collection.
Does PDF to PNG support transparent backgrounds?
Yes. PNG supports full transparency (alpha channel). If your PDF page contains transparent or semi-transparent areas, those will be preserved in the output PNG. This is one of the key advantages of PNG over JPG — JPG has no transparency support and will always render transparent areas as white.
Can I convert all pages of a PDF to PNG at once?
Yes. Every page is converted automatically when you upload your PDF. Once all pages are ready, click Download All as ZIP to save every PNG file in a single archive. For large PDFs, individual page download buttons are also available on each page card.
Why are my PNG files larger than JPG files of the same PDF?
PNG's lossless compression preserves every pixel perfectly, which results in larger file sizes compared to JPG (which discards some visual data to achieve smaller files). This is the trade-off: PNG gives you perfect quality at the cost of file size. If smaller files are more important than perfect quality, use our PDF to JPG converter instead.
