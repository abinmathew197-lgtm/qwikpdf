Frequently Asked Questions

Everything about converting PDF files to WebP images online for free.

How do I convert a PDF to WebP online for free?
Upload your PDF, choose your DPI and quality settings, then click Save as WebP on any individual page. You can also download all pages at once using the Download All as ZIP button. No account, no software, and no file upload to any server — everything runs locally in your browser.
What is WebP and why is it better than JPG or PNG?
WebP is a modern image format developed by Google that produces files up to 34% smaller than JPEG and 26% smaller than PNG at equivalent visual quality. It supports both lossy and lossless compression, full alpha transparency, and is now supported by every major browser including Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge. For web publishing, WebP is consistently the optimal image format.
What quality setting should I use for PDF to WebP?
For web publishing, 80–85% WebP quality is the recommended sweet spot — visually indistinguishable from higher settings at meaningfully smaller file sizes. Use 90–100% for high-quality archiving or when images will be viewed at large sizes. Use 60–70% for maximum compression when file size is the absolute priority and the content is primarily photographic.
Is WebP supported by all browsers and devices?
Yes. As of 2024, WebP has over 97% global browser support. All major browsers — Chrome, Firefox, Safari (since v14), Edge, Opera — and all modern mobile browsers on iOS and Android fully support WebP. It is also natively supported on macOS, Windows 11, and most modern image editing tools including Photoshop, GIMP, Figma, and Canva.
Are my PDF files uploaded to a server when converting to WebP?
No. All PDF to WebP conversion happens entirely in your browser using PDF.js. Your files never leave your device at any point — conversion, rendering, and download all occur locally. This makes it completely safe for confidential, legal, medical, and personal documents.
Does PDF to WebP support transparent backgrounds?
Yes. WebP supports full alpha channel transparency. If your PDF page contains transparent areas, they are preserved in the output WebP file. This is a significant advantage over JPG, which has no transparency support and renders transparent areas as white.
Why does Google PageSpeed recommend WebP over JPG and PNG?
Google's PageSpeed Insights flags non-WebP images as an optimization opportunity because WebP's superior compression directly reduces page load times — a key factor in Core Web Vitals scores. Smaller image files mean faster Largest Contentful Paint (LCP), which improves both user experience and Google search rankings. Converting PDF pages to WebP before publishing them on your site is one of the easiest ways to improve your site's performance score.
Can I convert all pages of a PDF to WebP at once?
